Polarization-Independent, High-NA Edge Detection with Nonlocal Metasurfaces

Yalong Liu, Xiaoxin Li, Bojian Shi, Qi Jia, Yanxia Zhang, Yanyu Gao, Wenya Gao, Fangkui Sun, Yongyin Cao, Rui Feng, Weiqiang Ding

Optics Letters (2025)
DOI: 10.1364/OL.562895

Summary:
A nonlocal dielectric metasurface for polarization-independent optical edge detection with both high numerical aperture and broadband response.

Highlights:
- Polarization-independent operation under arbitrary polarization conditions, including unpolarized illumination
- Broadband edge detection with a 48 nm operating range centered around 1064 nm
- High numerical aperture up to 0.5 for practical optical computing and imaging applications

Bilayer Metasurface-Enabled High-Sensitivity Near-Infrared Computational Spectrometer

Juntong Chen, Xiaoxin Li, Bojian Shi, Qi Jia, Yanxia Zhang, Wenya Gao, Yanyu Gao, Wentong Shi, Yongyin Cao, Hongyan Shi, Fangkui Sun, Rui Feng, Weiqiang Ding

Optics Communications (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.optcom.2025.132051

Summary:
A bilayer metasurface computational spectrometer that combines spectral encoding and broadband achromatic focusing for high-sensitivity near-infrared sensing.

Highlights:
- Ultra-compact footprint of only 21 × 21 μm
- Sensitivity improved by about 60% compared with conventional structures
- Spectral reconstruction fidelity reaches 93.5%

Efficient Coupling of Topological Photonic Crystal Waveguides Based on Transverse Spin Matching Mechanism

Bojian Shi, Qi Jia, Xiaoxin Li, Yanxia Zhang, Hang Li, Yanyu Gao, Wenya Gao, Xiaoyu Li, Donghua Tang, Tongtong Zhu, Shan Gao, Jing Yang, Rui Feng, Fangkui Sun, Chunying Guan, Yongyin Cao, Cheng-Wei Qiu, Weiqiang Ding

Nature Communications (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41467-025-59941-6

Summary:
A transverse-spin-matching mechanism for efficient coupling between topological photonic crystal waveguides and conventional strip waveguides.

Highlights:
- Reveals transverse spin matching as the key coupling mechanism for TWG-related interconnections
- Demonstrates theoretical and experimental transmission efficiencies of 96.3% and 94.2%, respectively
- Provides a route toward compact and high-efficiency topological photonic chips
